User Research

User research: summary

Throughout the project, multiple activities were planned and conducted starting with identifying the problem, discussing the same, identifying the user base and creating empathy maps for the same, and again discussing the goals and objectives discovered.
I also designed and conducted interviews in addition to usability study as well as competitive audit and came to numerous conclusions listing them all in the form of affinity diagrams, hence identifying priorities and insights and finally including all necessary details to form mockups and prototypes with planned presentations and interviews to check and revise the same.

User research: pain points

Accuracy: In detail and accurate information with links to in depth study documents and websites would be appreciated

Payment: Our users majorly wish to make all transactions , donations or other payments , via the app itself

Accessibility: Our visitors need information in a wide range of languages both audio as well as description

Intrusion: Our visitors would like non-intrusive information on command on favorable topics

Persona: Jeremy

Problem statement:
Jeremy is a college student who needs an audio guide for his date at the art gallery because he wants to enjoy the day with his boyfriend while relishing their mutual interests in art.

Persona: Camila

Problem statement:
Camila is a professor who needs an audio tour guide in English that satisfies her needs because she wishes to explore and enjoy the cultural and local art of the city at the gallery.

Refining Design

Other important stages

Listed are the other important stages undertaken into testing and development of the final prototype.

Usability Study: Conducting a moderated usability study gave me a deeper insight into the behavior, reactions, responses, and accessibility of the user concerning the app.

Goal and Problem statements: Observing the personas, goal and problem statements were created respectively.

Pattern and insight identification: Based on our affinity diagrams obtained after the usability studies a pattern and identification hierarchy was created.

User flow: The user flow helped me detect the solutions a user might need as they traverse through the gallery.

Competitive audit: To compare and improvise on the model created, a competitive audit was conducted to solve any additional issues and requirements.

Competitive audit report: With the outcome obtained from the audit, a report was created for the same.

Information architecture: This was an essential step in deciding the flow of the app.

Site map: Another tool for assessing individual issues with the app.

Accessibility Considerations

1. Accessible icons and alt text is provided plus major options such as map and profile are provided on the screen in addition to the icons in the bottom bar

2. The icons used are clear and easy to understand

3. Help option is provided on each page for any issues the user might face
